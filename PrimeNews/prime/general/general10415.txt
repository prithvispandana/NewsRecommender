HURGHADA, Egypt (AP) — The German government on Saturday confirmed that two German tourists were killed in a knife attack at a hotel in the popular Egyptian Red Sea resort of Hurghada. The German Foreign Ministry said it now has the "sad certainty" that two German female tourists died in Friday's attack. It didn't give any other information on the victims. In a statement, it said: "According to everything that we know, this act was aimed at foreign tourists — a particularly perfidious and criminal act that leaves us sad, dismayed and angry."
