Richard Whitehead led a glorious night as Great Britain claimed five gold medals on day two of the World Para Athletics Championships in London. After wheelchair racer Hannah Cockroft's gold on Friday's first day, Stef Reid won T44 long jump for her first major global title on Saturday morning. And Whitehead won T42 200m, Sophie Hahn triumphed over the same distance in the T38 class and Sammy Kinghorn won the T53 200m, while Hollie Arnold secured an emphatic victory in the F46 javelin to take the hosts' tally to six golds.
